Web and Distributed Programming

****Welcome to Week 1!

Welcome to week 1 of Web and Distributed Programming! I am looking forward to working with you to deliver high quality Web and Internet software applications. When I have asked students from previous sessions what the like most about this class and how we can improve it for future participants, the number one response I receive is almost always that they enjoy delivering something practical and useful while learning the underlying philosophy and theory.

As such, I attempt to lead off each week with just enough technical background to get you started, provide a practical (and hopefully useful & interesting) project for you to pursue, and then follow-up with more details on the tools and technologies that we are employing so that we can build on them in the following weeks.

Even in week 1 we will be leading off with installing development tools, writing HTML, and deploying Web applications even as we are learning more of the details behind our Web development technologies & tools like HTML, CSS, text editors, and cloud hosting.

Please make every effort to join our live discussion/lecture sessions on Wednesday at noon [join.me]. Grab your lunch and join us even if you can only stay for a portion of the time. I am looking forward to meeting you.

Although you are welcome to pursue our activities in any order you prefer, I am listing them in the order I think will be the most beneficial for you to work through the topics.
